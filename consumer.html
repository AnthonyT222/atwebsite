Consumer

A consumer is a software application that receives and processes data from a producer. Consumers can be used in a variety of contexts, such as in event-driven architectures, where they consume events produced by other applications or services.

Consumers can be implemented using various programming languages and frameworks, depending on the specific use case and requirements. They typically subscribe to a message broker or event stream, where they receive data in real-time or near real-time. Once the consumer receives the data, it can perform various operations on it, such as filtering, transforming, or storing it for later use.

Consumers play a crucial role in modern software architectures, enabling applications to react to changes in data and events in a timely manner. They are commonly used in scenarios such as real-time analytics, monitoring systems, and microservices architectures.
